Muay Thai: "The art of eight limbs"

Muay Thai, which translates to “Thai Boxing”, is the national sport of Thailand. Muay Thai has roots originating from military use dating back to around the 13th century. Muay Thai is known as the “Art of 8 limbs” because it makes use of 8 points of contact namely, punches, elbows, knees and kicks. Sign up to our Newsletter to learn more.

More about the sport

Stepping into this ring can be seen as going to war but is also seen as you having a strong heart.
Respect in this sport is unike any other. Massive appreciation goes out to the coach, family, and even opponent.
The headband worn in pre-fight attire is called the 'Mongkon'. It is given to a boxer after their trainer saw that the student had become an experienced fighter.
After the many pre fight rituals are over, like the Wai Kru, it is time for war!
